User Guide for SUPA Model Demo

This is an example for Economic Scenario Generator - SUPA (Simulation of Uncercentainties for Pension Analysis) Model to project uncertain future financial and economic outcomes. There are a total of fourteen economic variables that are projected, namely inflation q(t), wage inflation w(t), long term interest rate l(t), short term interest rate s(t), cash return c(t), domestic dividend yield y(t), domestic dividend growth rate d(t), domestic equity price return p(t), domestic equity total return e(t), international equity total return n(t), domestic bond return b(t), international bond return o(t), house price growth rate h(t) and unemployment rate u(t).

Functions and Features:

Features Description
Number of Years Slider Choose to show number of simulated years from 1 to 30
Number of Paths Slider Choose to show number of simulated paths from 1 to 50
Colored lines Check option to show colored simulated paths
Add Historical Data Check option to show data before year 2018
Display Data Points Check option to show data points
Show Percentiles Check option to show 5%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 95% percentiles
Recalculate New Paths Recalculate new paths for all economic variables
Play Animation Play/pause animation of the tooltip from year 1995 to end of simulated year


Simulation of variables:

You may move the mouse on canvas to show tooltips. The colored lines are simulated data formed with Monte Carlo Simulations.
The dashed lines are percentiles: , and

(Note: Percentiles are fixed from simulations of 100,000 paths.)

To refresh a page, press Shift-F5 in Chrome, or press F5 in Firefox. Then, it will recalculate new path of the SUPA Model.
